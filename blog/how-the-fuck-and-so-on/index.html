How the fuck to code faster and don't give a fuck

Let's face it, you're here because you've found yourself stuck in the quicksand of your own code, sinking deeper with every 'for' loop and conditional statement. Or maybe you've just overdosed on StackOverflow and you're experiencing the dreaded developer's block. It happens to the best of us.

You see, writing code isn't like popping tic tacs or brewing that morning cup of joe. It's a mentally torturing art, equivalent to threading a needle in a mosh pit.

One: Fuck the Perfectionism #

First things first, let go of that insatiable need for perfection. Newsflash – there's no such thing as perfect code. Yes, I said it. No golden statues or Nobel prizes will be awarded for the most exquisite use of a ternary operator. Trust me, I've tried.

Just look at the line above. Simple, right? But you could spend hours contemplating whether you should have used an 'if' statement instead. Don't. It's not worth it. We're not crafting sonnets here.

Two: The Art of Saying 'Fuck It' #

Writing code is all about solving problems, not creating new ones. Sure, your solution might not be the prettiest, but if it works and doesn't cause your computer to catch fire, then congratulations – you've done your job.

Look at the piece of code above. It's quicksort – a sorting algorithm that's as old as time. Not the most efficient solution, but it does the job. And that's all you need sometimes. Functionality over fashion. Code isn't a beauty pageant. Three: Red Bull, Coffee, and the Art of Not Giving a Fuck

If all else fails, there's always caffeine. But remember, energy drinks and coffee are not long-term solutions. They're like cheat codes for your brain – use them sparingly. The last thing you need is your heart beating like a drum solo at a punk rock concert.

In conclusion, coding is like life – it's a messy, chaotic, and unpredictable journey. Embrace the chaos, say 'fuck it' to perfection, and code like there's no tomorrow. Because in the world of software development, tomorrow is a luxury we don't have.

And remember, speed is not always about moving faster, but about wasting less time on bullshit. So cut the crap and just code.
