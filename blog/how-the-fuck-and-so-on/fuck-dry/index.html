Fuck DRY

Alright folks, next on my hit list is the DRY principle. Don't Repeat Yourself, they say. I mean, come on, who in their right mind thought this up? Probably someone who doesn't know the first thing about human beings. Because guess what? We LOVE repeating ourselves. That's how we learn. That's how we ensure things sink in.

DRY insists that 'Every piece of knowledge must have a single, unambiguous, authoritative representation within a system.' They're so terrified of redundancy they'll do backflips over a rainbow to avoid repeating even the slightest bit of code.

You ever tried telling a story or a joke without repeating yourself? You'd sound like a malfunctioning robot. 'So, I was walking down the street... oh wait, did I mention the street part already? Right, I can't repeat that. So, I was moving horizontally on a surface...'

And what about the glorious world of music? Last time I checked, it was called a 'chorus', not a 'grave error of repetition'. Maybe someone should tell these DRY fanatics that sometimes repetition is exactly what we need to drive a point home.

But, no! In programming, we have to treat repetition like it's some kind of unholy ritual. Can't have the same bit of code popping up twice. Oh no, the software gods might smite us! Let's instead spend countless hours abstracting that logic and introducing a million new bugs along the way. Because nothing screams efficiency like creating an extra layer of complexity to save typing out a couple more lines of code.

Don't get me wrong, I'm all for not reinventing the wheel. But hey, if I'm building a car and I need four wheels, I'm not gonna lose my mind because I'm 'repeating' wheels. Let's get real and quit treating DRY like it's some kind of sacred commandment from the coding heavens
